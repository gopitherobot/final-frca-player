What is the mechanism of oxygen toxicity? The proposed mechanism of toxicity is complex and not fully understood. However, oxygen derived free radicals are the likely etiological factors in toxicity development. Free radicals are produced as a result of mitochondrial oxy-reductive processes by xanthine oxidase at extra mitochondrial sites by auto-oxidative reactions and by phagocytes during bacterial killing. The free radicals cause lipid peroxidation in cell membranes, inactivate cellular enzymes, cause nucleic acid inhibition and protein synthesis inhibition. Under normal conditions, antioxidant enzymes such as glutathione peroxidase and catalyze and superoxide dismutase protect the body from these free radicals. But hyperoxic situations allow explosive free radical production that outstrips antioxidant capacity.
12:07

Hyperbaric oxygen is oxygen administered at higher than atmospheric pressure. It is used in carbon monoxide poisoning, decompression sickness, respiratory distress of newborn and anaerobic infections, for example, gas gangrene caused by claustridium welshy, refractory chronic osteomyelitis with draining lesions, infected superficial burns and to sensitize tumors to radiotherapy. The toxic effects are predominantly central nervous system effects. Under hyperbaric conditions, oxygen toxicity is again dose dependent and is the main limitation to treatment. It affects the central nervous system, lung, myocardium, liver, kidneys and visual system.
