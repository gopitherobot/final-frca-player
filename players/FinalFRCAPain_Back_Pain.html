Low back pain. How can we classify low back pain? Low back pain can be classified on the basis of duration of symptoms into acute and chronic. It is defined as acute when symptoms have persisted for less than six weeks and chronic when symptoms last for longer than three months. It can also be classified as specific and non-specific. Specific low back pain is caused by specific pathophysiological mechanisms such as herniated disc, infection, tumor, osteoporosis, inflammation or fracture.

How do you evaluate patients with low back pain? Think in terms of aims of evaluation and targeted assessment or examination rather than head to toe examination. The aims of evaluation include three things. Identification of red flags indicating serious pathology, identification of yellow flags for signs of chronicity and identification of source of pain. Assessments should include focused history, including the nature of onset, duration, location of pain, radiation, neurological symptoms including bowel and bladder function, exacerbating factors and relieving factors.
