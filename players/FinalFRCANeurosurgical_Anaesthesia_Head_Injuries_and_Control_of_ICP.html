Q5
What is secondary brain injury?
A
  • Secondary brain injury is the additional
  • neurological damage that happens after the primary injury. It occurs due to localized or
  • generalized physiological upset, particularly hypoxia and hypotension, but also hypercapnea,
  • intracranial hypertension, and cerebral artery spasm. These problems result in a scheme
  • here, disruption of the blood-brain barrier, and loss of the auto-regulation of cerebral
  • blood flow. At a cellular level, it is thought that the generation of free radicals are probably
  • responsible for cell death. This may be due to what is called excitotoxicity, a process
  • in which the excessive release of excitatory neurotransmitters, for example glutamate, cause
  • overstimulation of receptors leading to rising intracellular calcium. The calcium influx activates
  • a number of enzymes, including proteases, phospholipases, and endonucleases. These enzymes
  • then go on to damage cell structures, such as the cytoskeleton and DNA.
