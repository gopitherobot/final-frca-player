Q4
Can you classify epileptic seizures?
A
  • Epilepsy is defined as recurrent i.e. two or more epileptic seizures
  • unprovoked by any immediately identifiable cause.
  • A seizure can be defined as the clinical manifestations of an abnormal and excessive discharge of
  • neurons, which is seen as an alteration of consciousness, motor, sensory or autonomic events.
  • Seizures are classified based upon its clinical presentation and the electro-encephalographic picture.
  • They can be divided up into generalized and partial seizures.
  • Generalized seizures are characterized by diffuse symmetric brain involvement
  • and can be further broken down into inhibitory and excitatory seizures.
  • Inhibitory generalized seizures are absence or pity mal and atonic.
  • Exitatory seizures are the classic tonic, clonic and myoclonic variations.
  • In partial seizures there is seizure activity restricted to discrete areas of the cerebral hemispheres.

Tell me about fenetone.
A
  • Fenetoin is used for the prevention and treatment of generalized tonic, clonic seizures.
  • It is also used as an anti-arithmic and neuropathic pain modulating agent.
  • Its chemical class is a high dantuin derivative.
  • In its intravenous form it is presented as a clear colorless solution of a concentration of
  • 50 mg per mill of fenetoin sodium. The main actions are anti-convulsant and anti-arithmic.
  • Fenetoin works by inhibiting sodium and calcium influx during depolarization, thereby
  • exerting a membrane stabilizing effect. The intravenous loading dose for the management of epilepsy
  • is a slow bolus of 10 to 15 mg per kilogram. It is highly lipid soluble and peak brain levels
  • are achieved within 15 minutes. The maintenance dose is 100 mg 8 hourly.

A

  • Toxic doses of local anaesthetics can cause seizures. Tramadol lowers the seizure threshold

Q9
Which anaesthetic drugs are anti-convulsant?
A
  • Benzodiazepines are potent anti-convulsants at sub-anesthetic doses.
  • Fire pentone effectively terminates seizures in therapeutic doses.
  • Prophefall is anti-convulsant but may cause myoclonic jerks which mimic seizures.
  • The inhalational anaesthetic agents barring enfluorane are anti-convulsant.
