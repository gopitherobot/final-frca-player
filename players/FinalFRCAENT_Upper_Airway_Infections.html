So what is strider?
A
  • The examiners might ask an unexpected question to start with.
  • Try and stick to what you know and answer the question.
  • This is the harsh vibratory sound produced when the airway becomes partially
  • obstructed. It is caused by turbulent flow within the respiratory system.
  • The volume of the strider does not relate to the degree of airway narrowing.
Q2
Can you explain to me why children are more likely to develop strider?
A
  • Pediatric patients have small diameter upper and lower airways. A small reduction in the airway
  • causes a marked increase in resistance to flow as described by the Hagen-Poussai equation.
