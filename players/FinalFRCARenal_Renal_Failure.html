Renal failure. A 40-year-old woman presents for formation of an Arteovina's fistula to allow dialysis. She has established end-stage renal failure, secondary to diabetes. Describe your approach to the management of this patient. A structured approach is essential. Start off with an opening statement about the condition and then introduce how you are going to divide it up. Patients with renal failure have a spectrum of problems affecting all of their body systems. Renal failure can significantly affect the patient's fitness for surgery and there are some significant problems that need to be considered to keep a patient safe. A full preoperative assessment must be made with particular attention to comorbidities and biochemical state.
