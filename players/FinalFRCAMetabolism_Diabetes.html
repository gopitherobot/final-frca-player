How would you define diabetic ketoacidosis? Diabetic ketoacidosis is a state of insulin insufficiency that results in high blood sugar levels and the accumulation of organic acids in the blood. It is a serious, often life-threatening complication of diabetes and is characterized by the triad of hyperglycemia, metabolic acidosis and ketonemia.

What is the mechanism for ketone production and diabetes? This is testing knowledge of some of the steps in intermediary metabolism. You need at least be able to explain the final steps which lead to metabolic acidosis. Ketones are produced from acetile CoA in the liver mitochondria. Acetyl CoA is the end product of beta oxidation of fatty acids and the product of condensation of two pyruvate molecules. Acetyl CoA can be combined with oxaloacetate and then enter into the citric acid cycle of aerobic metabolism. In states of excess fatty acid breakdown such as diabetic ketoacidosis and starvation, then there is not enough oxaloacetate to combine with all of the astile CoA molecules.

The excess acetile CoA molecules are diverted into ketone production. The accumulation of the ketoacids, beta hydroxybutyrate and acetate are what result in the development of a metabolic acidosis.
