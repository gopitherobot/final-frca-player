Q8
Correct. Are you able to classify how you diagnose severe sepsis and septic shock?
A
  • A good examiner will always steer you to where points might be scored.
  • Diagnosing sepsis has recently been changed
  • from the CERS criteria documented in the 2004 surviving sepsis campaign.
  • CERS was not specific enough
  • and so an even more sensitive definition of sepsis has been adopted.
  • The guidelines are now more directed at severe sepsis and septic shock.
  • Yes, sepsis is defined as infection
  • with the systemic manifestations of infection.
  • These can be general variables including temperature, heart rate, mental state
  • or inflammatory variables beyond normal ranges
  • such as white cell counts and CRPs.
  • More critically in severe sepsis, there is organ dysfunction
  • including hypoxemia, oliguria, a significant increase in creatinine,
  • a coagulopathy, thrombocytopenia,
  • iliasaur hyperbilarubinemia or hyperlactatemia.
  • Septic shock is sepsis-induced hypotension
  • persisting despite adequate fluid resuscitation.
